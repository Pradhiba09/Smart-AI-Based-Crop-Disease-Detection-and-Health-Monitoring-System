# 🍎 New Crops Disease Guidelines

## Apple Diseases

### 1. **Apple Scab** 
- **Symptoms:** Brown/olive-colored spots on leaves, fruit with corky lesions
- **Severity:** ⚠️ High - Can cause significant crop loss
- **Treatment:**
  - Remove infected leaves and fallen fruit immediately
  - Apply fungicide (sulfur or mancozeb) every 10-14 days
  - Improve air circulation by pruning
  - Use disease-resistant apple varieties
  - Avoid overhead watering
- **Best Time:** Spring and early summer
- **Prevention:** Maintain good orchard sanitation

### 2. **Apple Black Rot**
- **Symptoms:** Circular black lesions on fruit, cankers on branches, progressive leaf damage
- **Severity:** ⚠️⚠️ Very High - Can kill branches
- **Treatment:**
  - Prune out infected branches and cankers
  - Remove dead/dying wood completely
  - Apply copper fungicide (copper sulfate)
  - Maintain good orchard sanitation
  - Burn or bury infected material
- **Best Time:** Winter pruning season
- **Prevention:** Sterilize pruning tools between cuts

### 3. **Cedar Apple Rust**
- **Symptoms:** Yellow spots with red rings on leaves, orange tube-like structures
- **Severity:** ⚠️ Medium - Mainly affects appearance
- **Treatment:**
  - Remove nearby cedar/juniper trees if possible
  - Apply fungicide during spring (before symptoms appear)
  - Use resistant apple varieties (Granny Smith, Liberty)
  - Improve air circulation
  - Remove infected leaves
- **Best Time:** Early spring (preventive)
- **Prevention:** Plant resistant varieties

### 4. **Apple Healthy**
- **Care Tips:**
  - Maintain balanced fertilization (N:P:K = 2:1:1)
  - Water consistently (1-2 inches per week)
  - Mulch around base (4-6 inches)
  - Prune annually for shape and air flow
  - Monitor for pests regularly
- **Optimal Conditions:** Full sun, well-drained soil, pH 6.0-7.0

## 🌽 Corn Diseases

### 1. **Cercospora Leaf Spot**
- **Symptoms:** Small gray spots with purple borders on leaves, spots coalesce
- **Severity:** ⚠️ Medium - Reduces yield 10-30%
- **Treatment:**
  - Implement crop rotation (3-year cycle)
  - Remove infected plant debris after harvest
  - Apply fungicide (mancozeb) if infection is severe
  - Plant resistant hybrid varieties
  - Avoid high nitrogen fertilization
- **Best Time:** When spots first appear
- **Prevention:** Use resistant hybrids, crop rotation

### 2. **Common Rust**
- **Symptoms:** Reddish-brown pustules on leaf surfaces, spores rub off easily
- **Severity:** ⚠️ Medium - Progressive infection
- **Treatment:**
  - Plant resistant corn varieties immediately
  - Remove alternate grass hosts around field
  - Avoid overhead irrigation (promotes spores)
  - Apply fungicide (propiconazole) in wet conditions
  - Increase plant spacing for air flow
- **Best Time:** Early in growing season
- **Prevention:** Choose resistant hybrids

### 3. **Northern Leaf Blight**
- **Symptoms:** Elongated gray-green lesions on leaves, becomes tan with dark borders
- **Severity:** ⚠️⚠️ High - Rapid spread in wet weather
- **Treatment:**
  - Plant resistant corn varieties
  - Practice crop rotation (corn ↔ legumes)
  - Remove infected plant debris completely
  - Apply fungicide (trifloxystrobin) weekly if wet
  - Avoid high nitrogen to reduce susceptibility
- **Best Time:** When lesions first appear
- **Prevention:** Resistant varieties, crop rotation, field sanitation

### 4. **Corn Healthy**
- **Care Tips:**
  - Plant at optimal spacing (24,000-30,000 plants/acre)
  - Fertilize with NPK 150-60-60 lbs/acre
  - Water 1-2 inches per week during growth
  - Weed control in first 6 weeks critical
  - Monitor for insect pests
- **Optimal Conditions:** Warm season crop, pH 6.0-7.5, good drainage

## 🍇 Grape Diseases

### 1. **Grape Black Rot**
- **Symptoms:** Brown circular spots on leaves, black shriveled berries with white spore masses
- **Severity:** ⚠️⚠️ Very High - Can destroy entire crop
- **Treatment:**
  - Remove all infected fruit and leaves immediately
  - Apply fungicide (mancozeb or sulfur) every 10 days
  - Improve air circulation through pruning
  - Remove dead wood in winter
  - Apply lime sulfur in dormant season
- **Best Time:** During growing season, preventive in spring
- **Prevention:** Proper pruning, good sanitation

### 2. **Grape Esca**
- **Symptoms:** Young leaves with yellow/reddish margins, woody tissue becomes soft/crumbly
- **Severity:** ⚠️⚠️ High - Progressive decline of vine
- **Treatment:**
  - Prune out infected wood immediately (disinfect tools)
  - Do NOT make large cuts (entry point for disease)
  - Apply wound sealant (Bordeaux paste)
  - Improve drainage and soil aeration
  - Avoid mechanical injuries to vines
- **Best Time:** Winter pruning
- **Prevention:** Avoid wounds, proper vineyard location

### 3. **Grape Leaf Blight**
- **Symptoms:** Small water-soaked spots on leaves, spots enlarge with concentric rings
- **Severity:** ⚠️ Medium - Defoliation reduces yield
- **Treatment:**
  - Remove heavily infected leaves
  - Improve air circulation (thin canopy)
  - Apply fungicide (sulfur or chlorothalonil) weekly
  - Practice crop rotation in vineyard
  - Remove debris around vineyard
- **Best Time:** Early in growing season
- **Prevention:** Good air flow, sanitation

### 4. **Grape Healthy**
- **Care Tips:**
  - Provide proper trellising/support system
  - Water 1 inch per week during growth
  - Prune in dormant season (winter)
  - Maintain soil pH 5.5-7.5
  - Annual fertilization with balanced NPK
- **Optimal Conditions:** Full sun, well-drained soil, moderate water

## 🌾 Wheat Diseases

### 1. **Wheat Leaf Rust**
- **Symptoms:** Small orange-red pustules on leaf surfaces, can appear on stems
- **Severity:** ⚠️ Medium - Can reduce yield 10-50%
- **Treatment:**
  - Plant resistant wheat varieties (check local recommendations)
  - Apply fungicide (propiconazole) at flag leaf stage
  - Remove alternate grass hosts
  - Improve field ventilation (avoid dense planting)
  - Monitor fields weekly during growing season
- **Best Time:** Early in infection, preventive during growth
- **Prevention:** Resistant varieties, host elimination

### 2. **Wheat Stripe Rust**
- **Symptoms:** Bright yellow-orange linear stripes on leaves parallel to leaf veins
- **Severity:** ⚠️⚠️ High - Rapid spread, severe yield loss
- **Treatment:**
  - Use resistant wheat cultivars (variety-specific)
  - Apply fungicide (azoxystrobin) at optimal timing
  - Monitor weather (cool, wet conditions favor disease)
  - Space plants adequately for air flow
  - Remove infected debris
- **Best Time:** Very early (before stripe forms), preventive
- **Prevention:** Highly resistant varieties critical

### 3. **Wheat Tan Spot**
- **Symptoms:** Tan/brown spots with yellow halos on leaves, eventually necrosis
- **Severity:** ⚠️ Medium - 20-40% yield reduction possible
- **Treatment:**
  - Rotate crops (wheat ↔ legumes/others)
  - Remove infected plant residue completely
  - Use tolerant/resistant varieties
  - Apply fungicide (mancozeb) if needed
  - Avoid excess nitrogen (promotes severity)
- **Best Time:** Preventive during variety selection
- **Prevention:** Crop rotation, resistant varieties

### 4. **Wheat Healthy**
- **Care Tips:**
  - Plant at 1.5-2.0 million seeds/acre
  - Apply NPK 80-60-60 lbs/acre (check soil test)
  - Provide adequate moisture (15-20 inches total)
  - Control weeds in early season
  - Monitor for insect pests
- **Optimal Conditions:** Cool season, pH 6.0-8.0, well-drained soil

## 🥒 Cucumber Diseases

### 1. **Cucumber Angular Leaf Spot**
- **Symptoms:** Angular brown spots on leaves, water-soaked appearance, yellow halos
- **Severity:** ⚠️ Medium - Reduces leaf area, affects photosynthesis
- **Treatment:**
  - Remove infected leaves immediately
  - Improve air circulation (better spacing, pruning)
  - Avoid overhead watering (wait until morning if needed)
  - Apply copper-based fungicide (copper sulfate)
  - Disinfect tools between plants
- **Best Time:** Early in infection
- **Prevention:** Avoid water on foliage, good air flow

### 2. **Cucumber Downy Mildew**
- **Symptoms:** Yellow spots on upper leaves, gray/purple fuzzy growth on undersides
- **Severity:** ⚠️⚠️ High - Rapid spread, complete defoliation possible
- **Treatment:**
  - Apply sulfur fungicide every 7-10 days
  - Apply chlorothalonil if sulfur fails
  - Reduce humidity (space plants, improve air flow)
  - Remove lower infected leaves
  - Avoid overhead watering
  - Plant resistant varieties if available
- **Best Time:** At first sign of symptoms
- **Prevention:** Resistant varieties, humidity control, air circulation

### 3. **Cucumber Healthy**
- **Care Tips:**
  - Plant 1 seed every 12 inches in rows 36-48" apart
  - Fertilize with NPK 10-10-10, side dress every 3 weeks
  - Water 1-2 inches per week, consistent moisture
  - Mulch to retain moisture and prevent soil splash
  - Harvest regularly to promote production
- **Optimal Conditions:** Warm season (60-85°F), full sun, rich soil, pH 6.0-7.0

## 📋 General Disease Management Principles

### For All New Crops:
1. **Early Detection:** Scout regularly, catch problems early
2. **Sanitation:** Remove infected plant material promptly
3. **Air Circulation:** Space plants adequately, prune for ventilation
4. **Watering:** Avoid wetting foliage when possible
5. **Resistant Varieties:** Always choose disease-resistant if available
6. **Fungicide Application:** Follow label directions, rotate products to prevent resistance
7. **Crop Rotation:** Varies by crop (3-4 year cycles recommended)
8. **Record Keeping:** Track disease incidence, treatments, and effectiveness

### When to Use Fungicides:
- ✅ **Preventive:** Before disease appears (weather conditions favorable)
- ✅ **Early Treatment:** Within 2-3 days of first symptoms
- ❌ **Late:** Once disease is severe, recovery takes longer
- **Rotation:** Change product classes every 2-3 applications to prevent resistance

### Environmental Factors:
- **Humidity:** >85% promotes most fungal diseases
- **Temperature:** Each disease has optimal temperature range
- **Rain/Dew:** Leaf wetness duration critical for infection
- **Wind:** Can help dry foliage (good) or spread spores (bad)
